SEO & Google Ads FAQ

Questions we hear from business owners every week

SEO (search engine optimisation) improves your organic rankings in Google — traffic that compounds over time at no cost per click. Google Ads (PPC) places paid listings at the top of search results immediately, but stops the moment you stop paying. Both work best together: Ads bring fast leads while SEO builds a long-term traffic asset.

Local SEO changes — Google Business Profile, citations, and location pages — typically show movement in 4–8 weeks. Broader keyword rankings take 3–6 months to build meaningfully. Competitive national terms can take 6–12 months. We prioritise quick-win opportunities first so you see early signals while long-term authority builds in the background.

The most common culprits are: wrong match types (too broad, attracting irrelevant searches), sending traffic to your homepage instead of a dedicated landing page, or no conversion tracking so you're flying blind. We audit all three as the first step of any Ads engagement and fix the landing page in parallel.

Page 2 stagnation is usually caused by one or more of: thin on-page content, weak internal linking, slow Core Web Vitals, missing E-E-A-T signals (author bios, trust markers, citations), or competitors with stronger backlink profiles. Our technical audit identifies the exact gaps and we prioritise fixes by ranking impact.

Rankings built through genuine optimisation — content, technical health, backlinks — don't disappear overnight when you pause. Unlike Ads, organic results persist. That said, competitors continue improving, so pausing long-term will result in gradual decline.
